Frequently Asked Questions

General

+
What is Juggar?

Juggar is a crowdlending platform that brings Lenders and Borrowers together. Borrowers will need to pledge at least a 30%1 collateral with cash or shares of Singapore-listed companies as collateral to safeguard the interest of the lenders.

Juggar is currently in development and is expected to launch in 2018.


1see illustration under Investor section.

+
What are the benefits of lending through Juggar?

The benefits include:

  • Receiving higher interest rates as compared to banks’ fixed deposit and Singapore Government Savings Bonds
  • Flexibility for the lender to determine interest rates and loan tenure
  • Lower minimum investment amount
  • Protection from downside risk given the high liquidity of collateral
  • High transparency as compared to other crowdlending alternatives like lending monies to private companies or purchasing credit invoices
  • There is no need for the lender to conduct due diligence on the borrower due to the quality of the collateral. The profiling of the borrower such as anti-money laundering measures as required by MAS will be undertaken by Juggar

Are my money guaranteed?

Juggar do not provide any guarantee that the Lenders will receive their capital back or achieve a targeted returns. However, Lenders are protected by the collateral put up by the Borrowers, which means your downside risk is minimised.

+
How are Lenders protected?

Borrowers are required to pledge at least a 30% collateral with cash or shares of Singapore listed companies. This roughly translate to 140% of loan amount (also known as margin ratio) which the borrowers have to maintain. That is to say if the price of these shares drop, Borrowers will have to top-up the difference with cash. Below is an example to illustrate:

Illustration Amount
Value of Collateral (shares or cash pledged) = $30
Amount of loan = $70
Total value of shares:
Value of Collateral + Amount of Loan ($30 + $70) 		= $100
Collateralized portion:
Value of Collateral ÷ Total value of shares ($30 ÷ $100) 		= 30%
Margin Ratio:
Total value of share ÷ Amount of Loan ($100 ÷ $70) 		≈ 140%

+
What if the Borrowers fails to top-up?

Juggar aims to bring benefit to all parties. If the Borrowers fail to top-up within the timeframe or if the value of the shares drops to less than 135% of the loan, the shares will be sold so that the losses do not eat into the Lenders’ capital.

+
What shares can Borrowers pledge?

Borrowers can pledge marginable shares determined by Juggar. The criteria for shortlisting of the shares will be dependent on the volatility, market capitalization and trading volume of the shares. In simpler terms, those deemed less risky are more likely to be on the list.

+
How safe is this 140% collateral?

Looking back at the Singapore stock market (Straits Time Index) as an indicator over the last 30 years, it has not fallen below 100% level such that the Lender will lose money.

The price performance of individual stocks within the Straits Time Index shows the same conclusion over the past 20 years.

+
Who will monitor the share price and ensure the Borrowers top-up with cash?

Juggar will administer the collateral and monitor the share price on behalf of the Lenders. This means we will do the margin calls and force-selling if necessary.

+
What happen if the share price drops such that the total value of shares is below the loan value?

In the unfortunate event that this happens, the Borrower is still obliged to repay the full loan amount plus agreed interest to the Lender.

+
How long is the tenure of the loan?

We are envisioning the tenure to be between 1 and 12 months.

+
Can I withdraw early?

No, money will only be repaid by the Borrowers at the end of the tenure. So chose your tenure wisely.

+
How much returns can I expect?

Currently, the rates for fixed deposit is around 1.25% and the borrowing rates for Borrowers is around 2.88%. We envision the interest rates to be in between these two rates.

+
Do I have to deposit my funds into Juggar first?

Yes, before Lenders can participate in the lending, funds have to be deposited with Juggar first so as to ensure the certainty of the funds to the borrowers upon a successful match.

+
How can Juggar ensure my deposited funds are safe until a match is being found?

Lenders will deposit their funds with a MAS regulated escrow agent.

+
Is Juggar licensed by MAS?

As Juggar is still in development stage, we are still working towards getting the required licenses from MAS.
